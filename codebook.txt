This codebook provides details for the variables included in the final output of the run_analysis script.
subject: The subject who participated in the experiment. Includes all those who participated, whether in the training or test group.
activity: A code to describe the activity that the participant engaged in that produced readings. Labels can be found in another variable, but the code is: 1 walking; 2 walking upstairs; 3 walking downstairs; 4 sitting; 5 standing; 6 laying.
timeBodyAccelerometerMean: This variable represents the body linear acceleration 3-axial raw signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
timeGravityAccelerometerMean: This variable represents the gravity acceleration signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
timeBodyAccelerometerJerkMean: The body linear acceleration for each direction was derived in time to obtain Jerk signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
timeBodyGyroscopeMean: This variable represents the angular velocity 3-axial raw signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
timeBodyGyroscopeJerkMean: The angular velocity for each direction was derived in time to obtain Jerk signals for X, Y, and Z respectively.
timeBodyAccelerometerMagnitudeMean: The magnitude of the body linear acceleration mean was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
timeGravityAccelerometerMagnitudeMean: The magnitude of the gravity acceleration mean was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
timeBodyAccelerometerJerkMagnitudeMean: The magnitude of the body linear acceleration jerk signal mean was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
timeBodyGyroscopeMagnitudeMean: The magnitude of the angular velocity mean was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
timeBodyGyroscopeJerkMagnitudeMean: The magnitude of the angular velocity jerk signal mean was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerMean: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the body linear acceleration 3-axial raw signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerMeanFrequency: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the weighted average of the frequency components for the body linear acceleration 3-axial raw signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerJerkMean: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the body linear acceleration jerk signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerJerkMeanFrequency: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the weighted average of the frequency components for the body linear acceleration jerk signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
frequencyBodyGyroscopeMean: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the angular velocity 3-axial raw signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
frequencyBodyGyroscopeMeanFrequency: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the weighted average of the frequency components for the angular velocity 3-axial raw signal mean for X, Y, and Z respectively.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerMagnitudeMean: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the magnitude of the gravity acceleration mean.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerMagnitudeMeanFrequency: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the weighted average of the frequency components for the magnitude of the linear acceleration.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerJerkMagnitudeMean: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the magnitude of the body linear acceleration jerk signal mean.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerJerkMagnitudeMeanFrequency: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the weighted average of the frequency components for the magnitude of the body linear acceleration jerk signal.
frequencyBodyGyroscopeMagnitudeMean: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the magnitude of the angular velocity mean.
frequencyBodyGyroscopeMagnitudeMeanFrequency: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the weighted average of the frequency components for the magnitude of the angular velocity.
frequencyBodyGyroscopeJerkMagnitudeMean: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the magnitude of the angular velocity jerk signal mean.
frequencyBodyGyroscopeJerkMagnitudeMeanFrequency: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the weighted average of the frequency components for the magnitude of the angular velocity jerk signal.
angle(timeBodyAccelerometerMean,gravity): an additional vector obtained by averaging the signals for the body linear acceleration 3-axial raw signal mean and gravity.
angle(timeBodyAccelerometerJerkMean,gravityMean): an additional vector obtained by averaging the signals for the body linear acceleration jerk signal mean and gravity.
angle(timeBodyGyroscopeMean,gravity): an additional vector obtained by averaging the signals for the angular velocity 3-axial raw signal mean and gravity.
angle(timeBodyGyroscopeJerkMean,gravityMean): an additional vector obtained by averaging the signals for the angular velocity jerk signal mean and gravity.
angle(X,gravityMean): an additional vector obtained by averaging the signals for the direction X and gravity. Also available for the directions Y and Z.
timeBodyAccelerometerStdDev: This variable represents the body linear acceleration 3-axial raw signal standard deviation for X, Y, and Z respectively.
timeGravityAccelerometerStdDev: This variable represents the gravity acceleration signal standard deviation for X, Y, and Z respectively.
timeBodyAccelerometerJerkStdDev: The body linear acceleration for each direction was derived in time to obtain Jerk signal standard deviations for X, Y, and Z respectively
timeBodyGyroscopeStdDev: This variable represents the angular velocity 3-axial raw signal standard deviation for X, Y, and Z respectively.
timeBodyGyroscopeJerkStdDev: The angular velocity for each direction was derived in time to obtain Jerk signal standard deviation for X, Y, and Z respectively.
timeBodyAccelerometerMagnitudeStdDev: The magnitude of the body linear acceleration standard deviation was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
timeGravityAccelerometerMagnitudeStdDev: The magnitude of the gravity acceleration standard deviation was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
timeBodyAccelerometerJerkMagnitudeStdDev: The magnitude of the body linear acceleration jerk signal standard deviation was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
timeBodyGyroscopeMagnitudeStdDev: The magnitude of the angular velocity standard deviation was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
timeBodyGyroscopeJerkMagnitudeStdDev: The magnitude of the angular velocity jerk signal standard deviation was calculated using the Euclidean norm.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerStdDev: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the body linear acceleration 3-axial raw signal standard deviation for X, Y, and Z respectively.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerJerkStdDev: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the body linear acceleration jerk signal standard deviation for X, Y, and Z respectively.
frequencyBodyGyroscopeStdDev: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the angular velocity 3-axial raw signal standard deviation for X, Y, and Z respectively.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerMagnitudeStdDev: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the magnitude of the gravity acceleration standard deviation.
frequencyBodyAccelerometerJerkMagnitudeStdDev: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the magnitude of the body linear acceleration jerk signal standard deviation.
frequencyBodyGyroscopeMagnitudeStdDev: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the magnitude of the angular velocity standard deviation.
frequencyBodyGyroscopeJerkMagnitudeStdDev: Fast Fourier Transform applied to the magnitude of the angular velocity jerk signal standard deviation.
activity_label: the activity being performed, in word form. There are six possible values: walking, walking upstairs, walking downstairs, sitting, standing, and laying.
